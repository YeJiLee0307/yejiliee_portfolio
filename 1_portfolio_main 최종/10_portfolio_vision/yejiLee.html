If I were to major in Art and Technology__

Developing App

I want to develop an app that helps users alleviate minor inconveniences in their daily lives. The app is tentatively called "MiriAlight" (a combination of "Miri" and "alight from a subway").

Inspiration: While commuting on the subway, I came across a statement saying, "Residents of Gyeonggi Province spend 20% of their lives on public transportation," and I wanted to address these everyday
inconveniences that people inevitably encounter. Given that there is a limited number of seats on the subway, passengers may have to stand for more than an hour at times. Predicting when seated passengers
will get off is a skill that students who commute develop. However, what if we could know when seated passengers are planning to get off? This could lead to a more efficient and less exhausting wait,
which is why I am interested in developing this app.

The app would assign a unique number to each seat in the subway car. When a seated passenger inputs their intended stop, other users sharing the same server would be able to see this information.
If such an app becomes commercialized, wouldn’t it lead to a better commuting experience?

Using Arduino

I became interested in this field after seeing an Arduino artwork at the exhibition "[What Do Objects Dream?]" at the National Museum of Modern and Contemporary Art. I found it appealing that,
through coding, it’s possible to go beyond mere implementation on a monitor and provide users with an experience that they can feel in a three-dimensional space. I want to take related courses
at Art and Technology and work on various projects.
